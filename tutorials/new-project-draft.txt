# 02. Microarray data analysis to identify DEGs in ovarian cancer.
<img align="left" src="/images/project-img/project1-mtb.svg"> **Background:** Comparing with other gynaecological malignancies, ovarian cancer-associated mortality ranks the highest. Due to the lack of deep understanding of molecular events in ovarian cancer, the pathogenesis of this deadliest malignancy remains unclear. In this study, we applied a bioinformatics approach to identify the upregulation and downregulation of significant gene after drug treatment to find out potential genes associated with the pathogenesis of ovarian cancer. 

**Materials and Methods:** To accomplish microarray data analysis for the identification of differentially expressed genes, the expression profile of GSE35972 was retrieved from the Gene Expression Omnibus (GEO) database. This dataset had a total of 6 samples of epithelial ovarian cancer cell, three of which was treated with NSC319726 and the rest of the samples remain untreated. This microarray dataset was investigated to obtain differentially expressed genes (DEGs) and deeply analyzed by bioinformatics methods. The Reactome2016 of Enrichr was used for pathway enrichment of DEGs. 

**Results:** Overall, 81 DEGs were identified from the GEO dataset and among these DEGs, 50 genes found as the upregulated and 31 genes as downregulated. Pathway enrichment analysis of Enricherâ€™s Reactome2016 shows the significantly upregulated genes are associated with the metal-binding activity, pyruvate metabolism RAS association and vitamin c metabolism as well as glycogen storage. Whereas the downregulated genes are mostly showing G2/M DNA replication checkpoint; crucial for cancer progression or regulation in a normal cell. Conclusion: This study indicates that screening for DEGs and pathways in ovarian cancer using integrated bioinformatics analyses could help us understand the molecular mechanism underlying the development of ovarian cancer, be of clinical significance for the early diagnosis and prevention as well as effective targets for the treatment of ovarian cancer. Keywords: Ovarian cancer, GEO data, differentially expressed genes, GSE35972, NSC319726.
